Tokyo

No trip to Japan is complete without a visit to Japan's capital! With so many wards and neighbourhoods to choose from, everyone is bound to find something that suits their taste. From busy areas like Shibuya and its legendary crossing to more traditional spots like Asakusa, Tokyo truly has something for everyone. Places like Shibuya, Shinjuku, Akihabara and Asakusa are only the tip of the iceberg, and it seems like possibilities are endless, so please explore Tokyo to your heart's content and find your own Tokyo!

Yokohama

The 2nd biggest city in Japan also has a lot to offer! Its proximity to Tokyo makes it an easy day trip destination. Between Yokohama Chinatown, which is the biggest one in Japan, the unique looking Red Brick Warehouse and Minato Mirai which will give you a look into the future, Yokohama is sure to surprise you. Don't leave too early, Minato Mirai is even more impressive during nighttime! And that's without mentioning some lesser known places in Yokohama like Sankei-en...

Osaka

Another extremely popular spot with tourists, Osaka's reputation for having great nightlife and street food options has been rightfully earned. If you want to get your okonomiyaki and takoyaki fix, Osaka is the place to go.

Nagoya

Often ignored by tourists in spite of being the 4th biggest city in Japan, Nagoya has everything you'd expect from a major city while retaining very high livability. It's very walkable and relatively free of crowds, however it doesn't mean there is nothing to do as plenty of interesting shrines and temples await you there, along with some lesser known areas such as Shikemichi. Nagoya is the perfect place to enjoy Japanese city living at a more leisurely pace.

Nara

When one thinks of Nara... The first thing that comes to mind is deer, and for good reason since they're quite ubiquitous. However, Nara's appeal goes beyond deer. Nara is a place shrouded in history and it being the first capital of Japan shouldn't be overlooked. With so many shrines to choose from and deer to fawn at, Nara is guaranteed to be hit on your next Japan trip. Pro tip: check out Naramachi for a slower, quieter Nara experience!

Kawagoe

Often nicknamed "Koedo" for Little Edo, Kawagoe shines with its traditional-looking streets. With Toki no Kane as a backdrop, you will feel like you just stepped back in time into a different era. An easy day-trip from Tokyo, it's a perfect place to enjoy cute cafes and lots of good food.

Kamakura

If you love temples and shrines, don't miss out on Kamakura! Kotoku-in and its great buddha statue is sure to leave an impression on anyone while other temples such as Hasedera will impress with their beauty. Make sure to get an Enoden ticket to get around the different points of interest!

Enoshima

Located in the city of Fujisawa in Kanagawa Prefecture, Enoshima is a charming island to visit, whether you do it alone or accompanied. Can be visited in a single afternoon as part of a Kamakura-Enoshima itinerary, however for a more enjoyable experience I would strongly recommend allocating a full day to this visit!
